What does "Unbanked" mean?

An unbanked person is someone who has never had a savings account, credit card, or any relationship with a formal bank or financial institution. In India, this includes farmers, daily-wage workers, street vendors, and rural populations — roughly 190 million adults. Without a credit history, traditional banks refuse them loans even when they are financially responsible. CredZ solves this by scoring them using alternative data — signals like how regularly they pay electricity bills, how long they've held a job, and their mobile usage patterns — instead of requiring a CIBIL score or bank statement.

Project Documentation

Full technical write-up — what CredZ is, how it works, and why it matters.

What is CredZ?

CredZ is an AI-powered credit scoring system designed specifically for India's unbanked population — people who have never had a formal bank account, credit card, or CIBIL score. Traditional lenders reject these applicants automatically, not because they're financially irresponsible, but simply because they have no recorded credit history.

CredZ changes this by scoring creditworthiness using alternative data signals — things like how regularly someone pays their electricity bill, how long they've held a job, or how responsibly they use their mobile data plan. These signals are combined using a machine learning model (XGBoost) trained on 18,429 real loan records.

Crucially, every decision is explained using SHAP (SHapley Additive Explanations) — so the borrower (and regulator) can see exactly which factors raised or lowered their score. This is not a black box — it's fully transparent, auditable, and compliant with RBI guidelines.

The Problem We're Solving
190M people excluded
India's unbanked adults are invisible to the financial system. They cannot get a loan, start a business, or handle a medical emergency through formal channels.
CIBIL score gatekeeping
Banks use CIBIL scores (India's credit score system) as the primary filter. If you've never borrowed formally, your score is 0 — and you're rejected, regardless of how responsible you are.
Predatory informal lending
Without access to formal credit, unbanked individuals often turn to local moneylenders charging 40–120% annual interest, trapping them in debt cycles.
How CredZ Solves It
Alternative data scoring
We use mobile usage patterns, utility payment history, employment tenure, and geographic data — all legally collectable with consent — to build a credit score from scratch.
XGBoost + SMOTE
Our model uses gradient boosting trained on 18,429 loan records. SMOTE oversampling balanced the class distribution from 84/16 to 50/50, raising default recall from 6% to 78%.
SHAP explainability
Every score comes with a plain-language breakdown of what factors affected it. This meets RBI's Right to Explanation requirement and builds applicant trust.

RBI Compliance — Plain English Guide

Most people building fintech AI have never read the RBI's guidelines. Here's what they say, why they matter, and how CredZ meets them.

What does "RBI Compliance Ready" actually mean?

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is India's central bank — it regulates all banks, NBFCs (Non-Banking Financial Companies), and fintech lenders. In 2021 and 2023, RBI released guidelines specifically about using AI and Machine Learning in lending decisions. These guidelines exist because AI models can discriminate, make unexplainable decisions, and harm borrowers if left unchecked.

When we say CredZ is "RBI Compliance Ready", it means our system is designed to meet these requirements — specifically around explainability, fairness, auditability, and data protection. This is not just a box to tick — it's what makes AI lending trustworthy.

The 5 Pillars of RBI AI/ML Compliance
1
Right to Explanation

What the RBI says: If an AI system rejects or approves a loan application, the lender must be able to explain the reasoning to the applicant in plain language. A black-box "the computer said no" is not acceptable.

Why it matters: Without explanations, a rejected borrower has no way to understand what they need to improve, and no way to dispute a potentially incorrect decision.

How CredZ meets this: SHAP values produce a per-feature explanation for every single prediction. The borrower sees exactly which factors raised or lowered their score — in human-readable language.
2
No Discriminatory Variables

What the RBI says: AI lending models must not use protected characteristics — gender, religion, caste, community, or race — as input features, either directly or as proxies.

Why it matters: AI models trained on historical data can learn and amplify existing social biases. A model trained on biased past lending data might learn to penalise certain communities without the developer even realising it.

How CredZ meets this: Our 123 model features contain zero references to gender, religion, caste, or community. The feature list is fully transparent and auditable.
3
Model Governance & Audit Trail

What the RBI says: Lenders using AI must maintain comprehensive records of how the model was built, what data it was trained on, how it performs over time, and a log of every decision it made — so regulators can audit it.

Why it matters: Without an audit trail, if the model starts behaving badly (e.g., rejecting more applications from a particular state), no one would know until significant harm had already been done.

How CredZ meets this: Every prediction is logged with its full SHAP attribution via the /api/history endpoint. The Analytics tab provides portfolio-level monitoring to detect score drift.
4
DPDP Act 2023 — Data Privacy

What the law says: India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP) 2023 — India's equivalent of Europe's GDPR — requires that personal data collected for AI scoring must be gathered with explicit consent, used only for the stated purpose, and protected from misuse.

Why it matters: Alternative data (mobile usage, utility bills) is personal. Using it without consent or selling it to third parties is illegal under DPDP and a serious breach of borrower trust.

How CredZ meets this: CredZ's design requires consent-based collection of all alternative data, uses data only for credit scoring, and does not store or share raw personal data beyond what is needed for the model input.
5
Human Oversight & Override

What the RBI says: AI models must not be the sole decision-maker for credit. There must be a human review process, especially for borderline cases, and the ability for a human officer to override the model's recommendation.

Why it matters: No model is perfect. Edge cases, unusual circumstances, and life events (medical emergency, natural disaster) may make a borrower appear risky when they genuinely are not. A human must be able to step in.

How CredZ meets this: The Medium Risk verdict is specifically designed to trigger manual review rather than automatic rejection. SHAP explanations give loan officers the context they need to make an informed human decision.
